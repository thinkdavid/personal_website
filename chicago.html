Chicago

Chicago is really a unique city. From the unique food, to the beautiful lake beaches, to the iconic architecture, there is a lot to see and do…in the summer. Can’t imagine what it’s like in the winter, except painfully cold.

Bars

  • Federales: Huge outdoor seating, with a good mix of music. Kanye played a couple times which was cool since he’s almost synonymous with Chicago. They have a bell outside that you can throw your shot glasses at after you finish taking the shot (make sure you order the ice shot though — there isn’t just glass everywhere)
  • Blind Barber: Didn’t get to go but was described as a cool, more laid back spot, apparently Chance the Rapper frequents it when he’s in town. Was recommended by multiple people
  • London House: Best rooftop bar in the city as far as views and aesthetic goes. There can be a line though and drinks are expensive. It’s a more bourgeois vibe but definitely worth a look.
  • City Winery on the Riverwalk: I liked this because it is literally just like a wine stand where you can get wine and then sit outside directly on the river. It feels very “open container” which is a cool vibe.
  • Kingston Mills: Blues bar in Lincoln park — cool because they play blues music, and it’s good. Kind of different, drinks reasonable priced, it was a good chill night.
  • Offshore Chicago - drinks were not good, and expensive, but the space itself is pretty cool. It’s a huge rooftop/patio type thing overlooking the pier and lake. It’s really nice up there.

Food

  • Portillos — OG Chicago Hot Dogs
  • Italian Village — good ass Italian food
  • Giardino’s — OG Chicago Deep Dish
  • Jack Melnyck’s Corner Spot - the wings slapped
  • Antique Taco - the tacos slapped

To Do’s:

  • Art institute of Chicago — really well designed museum with a bunch of different kinds of art, including the famous American Gothic painting (the old dude with the pitchfork and his daughter in front of a farm)
  • Museum of Contemporary Art
  • Millennium Park — this is where the Bean is (actually, it’s called Cloud Gate, apparently)
  • WNDR Museum — good for instagram
  • Signature Room at the 95th - great viewpoint of the city on the 95th floor of the John Hancock building. It’s free as opposed to the higher up viewpoint, you just feel peer pressured to buy a 15 dollar drink. Views are great though.
  • Rent a bike and ride along the lakefront. It’s a really easy ride, pretty flat, and beautiful. Plus you pass various beaches which are kind of cool views of the city.
  • Navy Pier — free to walk
  • Wicker Park - brooklyn-esque neighborhood, tons of graffiti and little quaint shops.
  • Museum of Science and Technology (I didn’t go but it was suggested)

Beaches

  • 31st Street Beach — this definitely the lit/black beach. When I pulled up (on my bike) there were hella people having cookouts, there was a Henny tiki bar, it was a good time
  • Ohio Street Beach —- closest beach to the downtown area. Always packed, pretty solid.
  • North Avenue Beach — A nicer beach than Ohio Street beach in my opinion. Less packed (but still packed), with nicer (and more) sand.
